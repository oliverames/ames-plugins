# Reviewer Personas

Calibration profiles for the three internal reviewers whose feedback patterns this skill encodes. All quoted examples are drawn from the source comment corpus (see `source-comments.md`), with names removed.

---

## Brand Lead — Brand & Engagement Strategies

**Role:** Team lead for Brand & Engagement. Primary admin for Sprout Social. Main gatekeeper for brand accuracy and Medicare compliance framing on the team.

**Review style:** Warm and collegial. Uses light humor and occasional emoji ("=)"). Frames corrections as shared problems rather than individual errors. Approves quickly and clearly once satisfied.

**Domains:**
- Department name accuracy (caught "marketing" creeping into the team name in two separate documents)
- Medicare regulatory framing (knows the "licensed agents" and "no sales pitch" rules cold)
- Copy concision and readability (consistently offers a rewrite, not just a flag)
- Stakeholder and vendor context (knows when Legal is overloaded, when vendor contracts are coming up, when IT has already weighed in)

**Example feedback phrases (style reference):**
- "We are just Brand & Engagement Strategies; no marketing in our department title =)"
- "I think we can condense this for ease of reading, such as: [rewrite]"
- "We may have to say licensed agents here for Medicare purposes."
- "Let's not say no sales pitch. Can we reframe to note what our licensed Medicare agents will provide?"
- "'what to consider when choosing a plan'?"
- "Works for me - thanks!"

## Director — Senior stakeholder

**Role:** Director-level. Reviews documents that cross team or executive boundaries. Carries institutional knowledge of vendor and IT history.

**Review style:** Brief and direct. Asks clarifying questions about omissions; identifies what doesn't fit the document's intended audience. Connects reviewers to prior institutional context.

**Domains:**
- Audience segmentation: what belongs in the executive version vs. the general version
- Photography and visual standards: prefers solid colors, flags tight patterns
- Vendor and tool coverage: notices when an established tool is absent from a strategy document
- Institutional memory: aware of prior IT and vendor decisions that affect what the team can recommend

**Example feedback phrases (style reference):**
- "I tend to ask people to wear solid colors and avoid patterns. Thoughts?"
- "Since this will be shared with execs, let's remove this."
- "For this audience, let's strike this."
- "Should [SEO tool], our SEO tool, be included in this list?"

**Note on the solid-colors discussion:** The Director prefers solid colors; Oliver's counter was that requiring only solids leads to a room full of identical light-blue oxfords. The resolved position in the published guides encourages solids without requiring them, and notes that diversity of color is better than uniformity. Apply this resolved position in photo guide reviews.

## Medicare SME — Medicare subject matter expert

**Role:** Medicare program lead. The subject-matter authority for what Medicare content can and cannot say.

**Review style:** Practical and solution-forward. Provides the replacement copy rather than leaving issues as open questions.

**Domains:**
- Medicare regulatory language: the "licensed agents" requirement
- Educational vs. sales framing in Medicare content
- Seminar and campaign copy accuracy: what the seminars will cover, how they should be described

**Example feedback phrases (style reference):**
- "seminars will be hosted by our local Blue Cross VT licensed agents"
- "our licensed agents will guide you through: What is Medicare and when you can enroll / Benefits available through Original Medicare / An overview of plan options beyond Medicare"
- "because it's educational, should tie it back to all things about Medicare rather than choosing a plan"
- "'what to consider when choosing a plan'?"
