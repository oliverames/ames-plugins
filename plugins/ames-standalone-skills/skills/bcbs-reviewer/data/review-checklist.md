## Photography and Visual Asset Review (Teresa)

Apply when reviewing a photo shoot brief, appearance guide, or event photography document.

**Clothing:**
- [ ] Solid mid-tone colors encouraged (navy, charcoal, burgundy, emerald, teal, plum) — but not required; diversity of color is better than uniformity
- [ ] Pure black and pure white discouraged (blowout under studio lighting)
- [ ] Tight repeating patterns flagged: thin stripes, small checks, herringbone (interference with camera sensor)
- [ ] Larger, low-contrast patterns are acceptable
- [ ] Suit jackets: appropriate for senior leadership; not a general expectation for all staff
- [ ] Vermont cultural context: flannel is acceptable for general-audience guides (not exec version)

**Audience version awareness:**
- [ ] Executive version: remove items appropriate for general staff only
- [ ] General version: may include Vermont-specific touches and informal dress latitude that exec version omits
- [ ] A companion doc for the other audience may exist; check whether a cut item belongs there

**Consent:**
- [ ] Member, partner, and guest photos require a release form — check with Community Relations or Brand & Engagement before the event
- [ ] Staff who want their image kept internal-only should tell the photographer before the session

## Medicare Campaign Checklist (Ashley + Jordan)

Apply to any Medicare-related social, event, or campaign content.

**Language:**
- [ ] "Licensed agents" or "licensed Medicare agents" — never "agents," "staff," or "our team" alone
- [ ] No "no sales pitch," "we won't sell you anything," or similar disclaimers — reframe to what agents provide
- [ ] Educational framing: "what to consider when choosing a plan," not "choose a plan" or "find the right plan for you"
- [ ] Seminar descriptions should say what agents will guide attendees through (use Jordan's formula: What is Medicare / when you can enroll / benefits available / overview of plan options beyond Medicare)

**Audience and platform:**
- [ ] Primary platform for 55+ Medicare audience: Facebook (not Instagram-first)
- [ ] Age targeting for paid: 55+
- [ ] Virtual seminars: Vermont statewide targeting
- [ ] In-person seminars: 25-mile radius from venue

**Campaign structure:**
- [ ] Single landing page as the destination for series-level awareness posts (not direct event links)
- [ ] Facebook Events are the RSVP surface: one per seminar, even when feed posts are grouped for the same window
- [ ] Feed posts are grouped biweekly (not one post per event) to avoid algorithm suppression
- [ ] Per-event boosts: $25 per event, approximately 2-week run ending on event date

## Event Photography Checklist (Ashley)

Apply when reviewing event photo capture guidance.

- [ ] Main 1x lens (no digital zoom)
- [ ] Lens cleaned before shooting (grease is the primary quality killer)
- [ ] Photos shared in original/full quality (not compressed)
- [ ] Flash off; available light preferred
- [ ] Burst mode for group shots
- [ ] Tap-to-focus on faces
- [ ] Complex steps: consider adding a visual or more explicit instruction if the step requires technical knowledge ("This may need some training or a visual added if a key step" — Ashley)
