# Reconciliation

Best practices for reconciling household bank and credit card accounts against YNAB.

## When to Reconcile

- **Weekly**: Quick check that YNAB cleared balance matches your bank's online balance
- **Monthly**: Full reconciliation against the bank/credit card statement at month-end
- **After big transactions**: Verify large purchases or payments posted correctly

## Reconciliation Process

### Quick Check (Weekly)

1. Open your bank app or website and note the current balance
2. Compare to the YNAB cleared balance for that account
3. If they match, you're good. If not, look for:
   - Transactions in YNAB not yet cleared at the bank (pending)
   - Transactions at the bank not yet entered in YNAB

### Full Reconciliation (Monthly)

1. Get the statement balance from your bank or credit card statement
2. Pull the YNAB account balance and all transactions for the month
3. Match transactions one-by-one between the statement and YNAB
4. Identify discrepancies (see categories below)
5. Resolve all items so both balances agree

## Common Reconciling Items

### Timing Differences (No Action Needed)

- **Pending transactions**: Charges authorized but not yet posted by the bank
- **Recent payments**: Payments made near month-end that haven't cleared yet
- **Deposits in transit**: Mobile deposits not yet credited

### Missing from YNAB (Add to YNAB)

- **Bank fees**: Monthly maintenance fees, wire fees, overdraft charges
- **Interest**: Interest earned (savings) or charged (credit cards)
- **Automatic payments**: Subscriptions, insurance, or bills on autopay
- **ATM withdrawals**: Cash withdrawals not recorded
- **Foreign transaction fees**: Fees on international purchases

### Incorrect in YNAB (Fix in YNAB)

- **Wrong amount**: Tip added after restaurant charge, or amount entered incorrectly
- **Duplicate entries**: Same transaction entered twice
- **Wrong date**: Transaction dated differently than bank posting date
- **Wrong account**: Transaction entered in the wrong YNAB account

## Credit Card Reconciliation

Credit cards have an extra consideration: the payment.

1. Reconcile the credit card statement balance to YNAB's credit card account balance
2. Verify the payment amount matches what was sent from checking
3. Check that the payment appears in both the credit card and checking accounts in YNAB
4. If you pay in full, YNAB's "Payment" column for the card should show the full balance

## Reconciliation Checklist

- [ ] Statement balance obtained
- [ ] YNAB cleared balance matches statement balance
- [ ] All uncleared transactions reviewed (still valid and pending)
- [ ] No transactions older than 7 days still uncleared (investigate these)
- [ ] Missing transactions added to YNAB with correct categories
- [ ] Duplicate or incorrect entries fixed
- [ ] Account marked as reconciled in YNAB

## Tips

- Reconcile regularly so discrepancies are small and easy to find
- Use YNAB's built-in reconciliation feature (lock icon) to mark reconciled balances
- If you find a small unexplained difference (a few cents), create an adjustment transaction in YNAB rather than spending hours hunting it down
- Keep bank statements (PDF or paper) for reference if questions come up later
