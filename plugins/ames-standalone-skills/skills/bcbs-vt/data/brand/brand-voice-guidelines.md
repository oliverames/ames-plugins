## Brand Personality

If Blue Cross VT were a person, they would be a trusted Vermont neighbor — someone who works at the local nonprofit, knows your name, shows up at the trailhead with hot chocolate, explains your insurance in plain language over coffee, and quietly donates to the food bank every year. They are frank about hard truths (healthcare costs, financial challenges) but always optimistic about solutions. They are never corporate, never distant, never jargon-heavy.

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Neighborly
- **We are:** Warm, local, personal, community-embedded — "nearly 400 of us live and work here"
- **We are not:** Corporate, distant, impersonal, or patronizing
- **This sounds like:** "We're Vermonters ourselves. So when it comes to delivering value, we get it."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "As a leading healthcare organization serving the Vermont market..."

### 2. Plain-spoken
- **We are:** Clear, jargon-free, educational — we explain complex insurance in everyday Vermont language
- **We are not:** Dumbed-down, condescending, or oversimplified
- **This sounds like:** "You can get cost estimates for repairs to your vehicle or house. Why should health care be any different?"
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Utilize our enhanced cost transparency module to optimize your healthcare expenditure."

### 3. Mission-driven
- **We are:** Purpose-centered, nonprofit-proud, always threading back to "making health care work better for Vermonters"
- **We are not:** Preachy, self-congratulatory, or performative
- **This sounds like:** "Giving our members immediate access to cost estimates is part of our mission to make health care work better for Vermonters."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Our corporate social responsibility program demonstrates our commitment to stakeholder value."

### 4. Candid
- **We are:** Honest about challenges, transparent about costs, direct about problems
- **We are not:** Defensive, evasive, or minimizing
- **This sounds like:** "We know how complicated and frustrating health care can be and how burdensome the high cost of care has been."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We continue to optimize our service delivery mechanisms."

### 5. Data-hearted
- **We are:** Evidence-based but always connecting numbers to human benefit
- **We are not:** Cold, clinical, or stats-for-stats-sake
- **This sounds like:** "In 2025, we paid $1.785 billion in member claims — that's approximately $4.9 million every single day."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Total claims expenditure for FY2025 was $1.785B."

## Core Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Vermont's Local Nonprofit
- 40+ years serving Vermonters
- No shareholders — dollars go to member claims and community
- 89.7% of every premium dollar goes directly to paying health care claims
- 6.2% admin expense ratio — lowest of any Blues plan nationally
- "We're Vermonters ourselves."

### Pillar 2: Community First
- Blue Sky VT Days seasonal outdoor challenges (Snow Days, Mountain Days, Kayak Days, Apple Days)
- Girls on the Run Vermont statewide sponsor
- $250,000 donated to 100 Vermont nonprofits (2025)
- Community Advisory Boards in Windham County, Northeast Kingdom, Rutland County
- Universal social mechanic: "Tag us → we donate $5 to [Vermont charity]"

### Pillar 3: Health & Well-being
- Free Be Well Vermont wellness platform
- Personal Health Support Team for chronic conditions
- 2,600+ in-network mental health therapists (8.4 per 1,000 members — 2x national average)
- 625,000+ telehealth visits in 2024
- Removed prior authorization for all in-state mental health services (2023) and ABA therapy (2025)

### Pillar 4: Transparency & Trust
- Cost estimate tool — shop and compare before care
- Formulary search for lower-cost drug alternatives
- Plain-language coverage explanations
- 2024 Call Center of the Year (SQM, selected from 500+ North American call centers)
- "Blue Cross Vermont will never call you to ask for personal information."

### Pillar 5: Vermont Healthcare Advocacy
- VT Affordable Care campaign (Dec 2025–present) with coalition partners
- "Vermonters Pay the Most in America for Healthcare" — 19.6% vs. 7.9% national average
- Consumer empowerment frame: "practical steps each of us can take"
- Never attacks competitors by name — the price data does the work
- CEO Beth-Ann Roberts: "all stakeholders come to the table to listen and collaborate"

## Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Crisis comms | Measured, factual, direct | "This is untrue and a misrepresentation of Blue Cross VT's financial condition." |
| Advocacy/policy | Urgent, data-sticker-shock, empowering | "Vermonters Pay the Most in America for Healthcare" |
| CEO communications | Frank, community-marshal, marathon-framing | "This is a marathon not a sprint." |
| Press releases | Professional, forward-looking, collaborative | Standard AP style with company boilerplate |
| LinkedIn | Text-forward, data-driven, zero hashtags | Leadership transitions, media appearances, community impact |
| Instagram | Warm, action-oriented, community-charitable | Blue Sky VT Days challenges, health education Reels |
| Facebook | Neighborly, outdoorsy, philanthropic | Event challenges + $5 donation mechanic |
| Member emails | Warm, practical, reassuring | Benefits explanations, enrollment guidance |
| Blog | Conversational, educational, empowering | Vermont analogies, named employee health stories |
| Internal comms | Collegial, direct, mission-connected | Team updates, cross-departmental coordination |
| Employer-facing | Professional but local, value-focused | "150 years of combined account management experience" |

### Five Tonal Modes

1. **Empowering Educator** — "Here's what you have. Here's how to use it." (cost tool, formulary, Be Well VT)
2. **Trusted Neighbor** — "We're from Vermont. We're not going anywhere. We're rolling up our sleeves alongside you." (community events, member letters)
3. **Evidence-Led Advocate** — "19.6% vs. 7.9%. This is not sustainable." (Affordability Matters, VT Affordable Care)
4. **Human Storyteller** — Name + title + their health moment. No jargon. Real employees, real members. (Andrew, Jennie, Marcey)
5. **Celebratory Community Partner** — "25 years. Here's what that means to the people who lived it." (GOTR, Blue Sky VT Days recaps)

## Terminology

### Preferred Terms

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| members | customers, consumers | Always "members" and "Vermonters" |
| older Vermonters | seniors | In both boilerplate and body copy |
| health care | healthcare | Two words (AP style) — except in campaign names like "VT Affordable Care" |
| Blue Cross VT / Blue Cross Vermont | the company | Never use "the company" in member-facing copy |
| make health care work better | optimize health outcomes | Plain language always |
| Vt. | VT | In running press release text (AP style) |

### Never Write

- Numbered-list headlines ("7 Ways to...")
- Clickbait or fear-based health messaging
- "synergy," "leverage," "utilize," "optimize"
- Attack competitors or hospitals by name
- Medicare Advantage references — Vermont Blue Advantage discontinued all MA plans in 2026
- Corporate distancing language

### Brand Vocabulary

**Identity anchors:**
- "the state's only local, nonprofit health plan"
- "for more than 40 years"
- "more than one third of Vermonters" / "more than 200,000 Vermonters"
- "Vermonters Serving Vermonters"

**Proximity language:**
- "alongside Vermonters"
- "friends and neighbors across the state"
- "rolling up our sleeves"
- "we show up for one another"

**Mission language:**
- "make health care work better for Vermonters"
- "removing barriers"
- "a lifeline to navigating the healthcare journey"

**Advocacy language (high-stakes only):**
- "we have an affordability crisis"
- "Not for us. Not for Vermonters. Not for the future of care."
- "Because Affordability Matters."
- "This is a marathon not a sprint."

## Channel-Specific Rules

### LinkedIn (@bluecrossvt | 5,188 followers)
- **Zero hashtags** on standard posts (2025–2026 confirmed). Exception: observance hashtags (#HeartHealthMonth) occasionally.
- Posts are text-forward, 2–4 sentences max, with one external link
- Leadership transitions (welcomes/farewells) are the highest-performing content by far
- CEO media appearances second, member/employee health stories third

### Instagram (@bluecrossvt | 1,510 followers)
- Hashtags go in the **first comment**, not the caption body
- CTA ends with "at the link in our bio"
- Emoji: contextual, 1–3 per post (❄️🍎🍂💙)
- Partner orgs always @-tagged
- Top-performing: Financial/practical tools with clear action step (HSA/FSA Reel = 39 likes, all-time high)

### Facebook (@BlueCrossVT)
- Primary mechanic: "Tag Blue Cross Vermont → we donate $5 to [Vermont charity partner]"
- Slightly longer captions OK (2–5 sentences)
- Event promotion + community spotlights + blog links
- Respond to comments within 24 business hours

### Blog (Be Well VT)
- Tagline: "Empower your wellness journey with the latest health insights, inspiring stories, and essential insurance tips."
- Standard structure: Hook → Context → Named expert quote → Subheaded body → Tools/links → Warm closing CTA
- Headline patterns: Sentence case, action-oriented, specific (not numbered lists)
- The "Vermont analogy" is a signature opening device (firewood stacks, maple syrup, car repairs)
- Named employees as protagonists is the strongest engagement format (~30% of posts)

### Email
- Subject lines: Under 50 characters, specific, Vermont-relevant
- Salutation: "Hi [First Name]" for member; "Dear [Name]" for formal
- Footer: Unsubscribe link, physical address (Berlin, VT), licensee line

### Press Releases
- AP style throughout
- Dateline: Berlin, Vt., [Month Day, Year]
- Always end with company boilerplate (see below)
- Titles: capitalize before name, lowercase after
