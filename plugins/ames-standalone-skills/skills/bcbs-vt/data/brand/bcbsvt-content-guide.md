# BCBS VT Content Creation Guide

## Organization Context
Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont — Vermont's only local, not-for-profit health plan. 230,000+ members. Social Media Strategist: Oliver Ames.

**Brand guidelines:** Load from `~/.claude/brand-voice-guidelines.md` for full voice, terminology, and channel rules.

## Content Patterns That Work

### Blog Posts (Be Well VT)
- **Vermont analogy opening** (signature device): Ground complex insurance concepts in Vermont life
  - "You can get cost estimates for repairs to your vehicle or house. Why should health care be any different?"
  - "Vermonters know that some things are just better when they are stacked. Firewood dries faster in stacks..."
- **Named employee/member as protagonist** (~30% of posts, strongest engagement):
  - Formula: specific person + specific moment + emotional honesty + health lesson + practical steps
  - Always include: full name, job title, specific health condition
- **Standard structure:** Hook → Context → Named expert quote (with title) → Subheaded body → Specific tools/links/phone numbers → Warm closing CTA
- **Headlines:** Sentence case, action-oriented, never numbered lists ("7 Ways to..." is NOT BCBS VT)

### Social Media
**LinkedIn** (5,188 followers):
- ZERO hashtags (2025–2026). Text-forward, 2–4 sentences, one external link.
- Top content: Leadership transitions (welcomes/farewells) > CEO media appearances > employee health stories
- David Yoo CIO welcome = 312 likes/113 comments (all-time high)

**Instagram** (1,510 followers):
- Hashtags in FIRST COMMENT only, never caption
- End with "at the link in our bio"
- Emoji: contextual, 1–3 per post (❄️🍎🍂💙)
- Top performer: HSA/FSA Reel (39 likes) — financial/practical tools win

**Facebook:**
- Primary mechanic: "Tag us → $5 donation to [Vermont charity]"
- Seasonal Blue Sky VT Days challenges drive engagement

### Email
- Subject lines: Under 50 characters, specific, Vermont-relevant
- Salutation: "Hi [First Name]" for member emails
- Always include: phone number, bluecrossvt.org, licensee line in footer

### Press Releases
- AP style: "health care" (two words), "Vt." (not VT), spell out percent in text
- Always end with company boilerplate (verbatim in brand guidelines)
- Dateline: Berlin, Vt., [Month Day, Year]

## Recurring CTAs (Use Verbatim)
- "We hope this article has helped you better understand your [benefit], and that you'll use this information to maximize your benefits."
- "Call us at (800) 247-2583 (TTY: 711), Monday to Friday from 7:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m."
- "Because Affordability Matters."
- "Let's keep going."

## Key Statistics for Content (Update Annually)
- 89.7% of every premium dollar → health care claims
- $1.785 billion in claims paid (2025) — ~$4.9 million/day
- $250,000 donated to 100 VT nonprofits (2025)
- VT households spend 19.6% of income on healthcare (vs. 7.9% national — #1 most expensive)
- 2,600+ in-network mental health therapists (8.4 per 1,000 members — 2x national avg)
- 625,000+ telehealth visits in 2024

## Never Write
- "customers" or "consumers" — always "members" and "Vermonters"
- "seniors" — always "older Vermonters"
- Medicare Advantage references (discontinued 2026)
- Numbered-list headlines
- "synergy," "leverage," "utilize," "optimize"
- Attack competitors by name
