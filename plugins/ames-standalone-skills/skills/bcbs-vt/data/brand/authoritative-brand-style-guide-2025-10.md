## Brand Story

As Vermont's only local, non-profit health plan, Blue Cross VT is driven to contribute to the state's authentic way of life with reliable, high-quality health care. With more than 400 employees living and working in Vermont, we help neighbors navigate the complexities of health care.

**Brand promise:** Vermonters making healthcare work better for Vermonters.

**Vision:** Together, we can build a transformed health care system in which every Vermonter has healthcare coverage and receives timely, effective, affordable care.

**Mission:** We are committed to the health of Vermonters, outstanding member experiences, and responsible cost management for all the people whose lives we touch.

## Brand Attributes

- **Functional attributes:** ethical, flexible, innovative, trustworthy — speak to our products and services
- **Emotional attributes:** inclusive, supportive, thoughtful — express how people feel about us

**Brand personality: The Everyman Caregiver.** All for one and one for all. A moral obligation, motivated by mission to help others. Reliable, utmost integrity, not extravagant. Customers appreciate simple things and genuine experiences.

## Core Values

- Customer-driven excellence
- Focus on the future
- Valuing each other and our partners
- Integrity, public responsibility, and citizenship

## Communication Pillars

1. **Your Health Is Our Singular Focus** — passion and purpose to provide high-quality care and coverage for all Vermonters as a local nonprofit.
2. **An Exceptional Experience** — treat members like neighbors, offering trusted guidance and support for total well-being.
3. **Adding Greater Value Every Day** — help members get the most from every dollar with flexible plan options and practical tools.
4. **We Are Authentic Vermont** — born in Vermont in 1989, the state's only local, non-profit health plan; 400+ employees who value the trust their neighbors place in them.

## Approved Names (CRITICAL — do not improvise)

**Approved:**
- Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont
- Blue Cross VT

**Not approved:**
- ❌ Blue Cross of Vermont
- ❌ Blue Cross Vermont
- ❌ BCBS Vermont
- ❌ BCBSVT

**Registration indicia (®):** Apply the ® to the name at the first or most prominent appearance of a mark. Subsequent mentions in sentence text or headers can appear without registration marks.

**Internal references:** Use "Blue Cross," "we," "our," "our company," or "our organization" for internal messages.

## Tone of Voice (six attributes)

| Attribute | How we apply it |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Clear and Direct | Sixth-grade reading level or lower |
| Truthful | Honest and transparent |
| Friendly and Polite | Use "we" and "our" for connection |
| Empathetic | Listen and meet people where they are |
| Inspiring | Be positive, helpful, and empowering |
| Uniquely Vermont | We are Vermont's only local health plan |

**Our writing should not:**
- Use complex, hard-to-understand language above a sixth-grade reading level, jargon, or a corporate tone
- Include vague, exaggerated, or unclear claims instead of honest, transparent information
- Stray from our brand by using incorrect names, generic messaging, or language that doesn't reflect our identity

## Logos

**Logo anatomy:** Symbols + Name + Tagline ("An Independent Licensee of the Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association").

**Four approved formats:** Horizontal · Centered · Left aligned · Stacked. Preferred: blue symbols with black text.

**Alternative logos** (for dark/blue backgrounds or B&W printing): Blue and white · White · Black.

**When the tagline is required:** All logo uses except where the tagline already appears as a footnote, radio/TV/web ads, outdoor ads (billboards), ads smaller than 3"×3", business cards, envelopes, and promotional items.

**Sizing minimums:**
- Print: Blue Cross symbol must be at least ¼" tall
- Screens: at least 30 pixels tall

**Unacceptable uses (never):** blurry · stretched · color-changed · rearranged symbol and name · cut off · symbol blending into background · tilted · unreadable on background.

**Product and program logos:** Vermont Blue RX. Blue Distinction® Center — Cardiac Care (same format across all Blue Distinction specialties).

**Logo requests:** All logo files must be requested via email from Brand and Engagement Strategies. Include where/how the logo will be used and provide a digital proof for approval.

## Typography

**Primary font (marketing):** **DIN** — use for all official communications, member-facing materials, and digital assets.

**Secondary font (internal):** **Calibri** — for everyday tools like Microsoft Word, PowerPoint, and Excel that don't support DIN natively.

**Font sizes:**
- **Minimum 12 point** for body text
- **14 to 18 point** recommended for accessibility / poor vision
- Larger sizes for titles and headings

**Font style:**
- Don't use **all caps** for long sections — reserve for short titles or headers
- Use **bold sparingly**: limit to headings or key words, not full sentences or paragraphs
- Use **light weight** for headlines at 24 point or larger

> Note: The Writing and Tone Style Guide (Aug 2021 / rev Nov 2021) specifies **11 point** body text, 12 point for Medicare. Where there is a conflict, the newer Brand Style Guide (Oct 2025) 12 pt minimum takes precedence for brand-general communications. For Medicare, always follow 12 pt plus the CMS regulatory review process.

## Photography

**Photo story:** Vermonters in beautiful Vermont landscapes — adventurous spirit, commitment to healthy pursuits, deep connection to the natural environment.

**Image selection — choose images that:**
- Appear candid, capturing a real moment
- Feel authentic
- Represent diverse Vermonters
- Show care that feels positive and genuine

**Avoid:** washed-out backgrounds · unclear focus · blurry people · unprofessional angles · posed models · illustrated imagery / clip art.

## Visual Style

**Signature mountains graphic** appears across marketing channels to create a connected brand experience. Reflects Vermont roots and signals the message is from Blue Cross VT.

**Templates:** Download from the Brand Resource Center for agendas, PowerPoint, email signatures, and more.

**Icons:**
- Select familiar, consistent icons (one per concept — e.g., one bill-pay icon, not multiple)
- Not everything needs an icon — too many reduces scannability
- Pair every icon with a short text label
- Leave space around icons for visibility
- Use even lines and brand colors with contrast
- When placing a shape behind an icon, use a circle (not a square)

## Experience Design

**Design for accessibility — design tips for clarity:**
- Visual hierarchy: up to 3 text sizes (titles, subheadings, body)
- Highlight with bold, bullets, or numbered lists
- Vary phrasing: don't start every bullet the same way
- Group related content; one theme per section
- Consistent calls to action with verb starts ("sign up," not "enrollment")
- Balance content with white space

**Digital accessibility requirements:**
- Support screen readers, keyboard navigation, text resize with browser zoom
- Descriptive link text ("Find a doctor" — not "Click here")
- Alt text for all visuals
- WCAG 2.0 Level AA color contrast (verify with WebAIM Contrast Checker)

## Co-Branding

Co-branded communications involve Blue Cross VT alongside an unlicensed entity (vendor, affiliate, or service provider). Two types: Strategic Level (high visibility, rare) and Premier Level (more common). Both require **formal approval**.

**Build 6–8 weeks** into the project timeline for approval. Approval is valid for three years; re-apply within that time frame.

**Premier-level eligibility standards** (partner must meet):
- Not on the Brand Conflict List or a National Competitor
- Good financial standing
- No felony convictions in the past three years
- Positive reputation and business practices

**Co-branding guidelines:**
- Blue Cross VT must be equal to or more prominent than the partner's name/logo in all materials (size, color, placement, visual weight)
- Co-branded content must focus exclusively on services that support the Blue-branded product; may not promote unrelated services
- Must clearly disclose the independent status of the partner and their role: "[Partner Name] is an independent company providing [service] on behalf of Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont"
- Must NOT imply the partner is licensed by BCBSA, affiliated with a Licensee, or part of the Blue Cross Blue Shield System
- Language must NOT imply endorsement, sponsorship, or partnership (avoid "endorsed by" or "in partnership with")

**Within six months of use**, submit samples of co-branded materials to BCBSA via Brand and Engagement Strategies for compliance review.

## Derivative Marks

Derivative marks are any words, phrases, names, domain names, designs, or symbols incorporating or used in combination with Blue, Cross, Shield, BC, BS, BCBS, the Blue Cross or Blue Shield symbols, or any non-Blue BCBSA mark (e.g., Blue Edge Classic℠).

**BCBSA owns all derivative marks.** Formal BCBSA approval is required before use. Derivative marks must be resubmitted every three years to remain active.

**Requests must include:**
- Description of the derivative mark and its format
- Description of how the products/services will be used
- Identification of the licensee(s)

**BCBSA will not approve a derivative mark if:**
- Use would likely mislead or confuse the public about the nature of the product/service
- Non-Blue distinguishing word/symbol is displayed more prominently than the most prominent use of the brands
- Use would imply a claim of superiority or exclusivity when the service area is shared by more than one licensee
