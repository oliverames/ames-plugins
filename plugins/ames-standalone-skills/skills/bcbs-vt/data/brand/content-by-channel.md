# BCBS VT Content by Channel

## Website Content

### Voice
Authoritative, plain-spoken, member-focused. Pages serve as the primary source of truth.

### Structure Rules
- **Headlines:** Sentence case. Action-oriented. Specific.
  - ✅ "Three ways to lower your prescription costs"
  - ❌ "Prescription Drug Information"
- **Intro paragraphs:** Lead with member benefit, not organizational description
- **Body:** Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences). Use subheads generously.
- **Links:** Descriptive anchor text ("Learn how to use the cost tool") not "click here"
- **CTAs:** One primary CTA per page. "Connect with Us," "Read our blog," "Get a quote."

### SEO Considerations
- Primary keyword placement: headlines, first paragraph, URL slug
- Plain-language terms match how Vermonters search ("Vermont health insurance," "Blue Cross VT")
- Include Vermont geography where relevant

### Disclaimer Language
- "An Independent Licensee of the Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association" on all pages
- Medicare pages: Required CMS disclaimer language
- Coverage details: "Benefits vary by plan; check your specific plan for details"

## Email

### Formats
1. **Member newsletters** — Monthly health tips, upcoming events, benefit reminders
2. **Transactional / administrative** — Claims, enrollment, ID card, billing
3. **Marketing / acquisition** — Plan comparisons, enrollment periods, employer outreach
4. **Internal employee communications** — Culture, HR updates, team announcements

### Voice Rules
- Subject lines: Under 50 characters. Specific, not clickbait. Vermont-relevant when possible.
  - ✅ "Your 2026 wellness benefit — here's how to claim it"
  - ❌ "Important information about your account"
- Preheader text: Expand on subject, don't repeat it
- Salutation: "Hi [First Name]" for member emails; "Dear [Name]" for formal correspondence
- Signature: Include name, title, phone, email, bluecrossvt.org
- Footer: Unsubscribe link, physical address (Berlin, VT), "An Independent Licensee of the Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association"

### Tone by Email Type
| Type | Tone |
|------|------|
| Health tips | Warm, encouraging, practical |
| Administrative | Neutral, clear, reassuring (no alarm) |
| Marketing | Confident, benefit-forward |
| Crisis/sensitive | Factual, calm, supportive |
| Internal | Collegial, direct, mission-connected |

## Social Media

### Platform Strategy

**Instagram (@bluecrossvt)**
- Visual-first: Vermont landscapes, community events, employee spotlights, wellness tips
- Captions: 1–5 sentences + "link in bio" CTA. Keep conversational and warm.
- **Hashtags go in the FIRST COMMENT, not the caption body** (confirmed from live posts)
- Hashtag count: 5–12 in comment (event-specific + general: #BlueCrossVT #Vermont #vermontlife)
- Confirmed event hashtag: **#snowdaysvt** (Snow Days). Other events use account tag only (@bluecrossvt).
- Stories: Behind-the-scenes, quick wellness tips, event day coverage
- Reels: Financial wellness education performs best (HSA/FSA = 39 likes, account high); recipe/lifestyle also used
- Top-performing format: Clear action + Vermont community impact ("Tag us → we donate $5 to @VermontFoodbank")
- Emoji: Contextual, 1–3 per post (❄️🍎🍂💙). Never decorative stacks.
- Some posts marked "Edited" after publishing — normal practice
- Story Highlights maintained: "Local Discounts" | "Insurance 101"

**Facebook (facebook.com/BlueCrossVT)**
- Broader audience; slightly longer captions OK (2–5 sentences)
- Primary content: Blue Sky VT Days seasonal challenges, event promotion, community spotlights, blog links
- Core engagement mechanic: "Tag us → $5 donation to [charity partner]" — lead with this for all seasonal events
- Share news coverage when BCBS VT is featured; respond to comments within 24 business hours
- Emoji: Yes, contextual (same as Instagram)
- Hashtags: Event-specific only (e.g., #snowdaysvt); not used on standard posts

**LinkedIn (linkedin.com/company/bluecrossvt/)**
- Most professional tone; B2B + employer audience + media + policymakers
- **ZERO hashtags on current posts (2025–2026)** — confirmed from live account. Posts are text-forward with external links.
  - Exception: observance hashtags (#HeartHealthMonth, #CommunityHealth) appear occasionally
- Data-forward: specific metrics, company milestones, financial transparency
- Highest-performing content: Leadership transitions (new hires, retirements) → activate personal networks
  - David Yoo welcome post: 312 likes, 113 comments (all-time high)
  - Don George farewell: 167 likes, 15 comments (recent high)
- Second tier: CEO media appearances, employee/member personal health stories
- Low-performing: Seasonal events, operational announcements
- Post length: 2–4 sentences + link. Do not pad.

**YouTube (youtube.com/bcbsvt)**
- Confirmed video: "Be Well Vermont Overview Video" (platform walkthrough for members)
- TRAFFIK campaign "Keep Vermont Well" produced TV/video content (Heart of the Community video)
- Instagram Reels are the primary short-video channel — YouTube appears underutilized
- Titles: SEO-optimized plain language

### Social Voice
- Warm, community-proud, encouraging
- Never negative about competitors or political opponents
- Celebrate Vermonters by name (with permission)
- Use "we" — the organization has a personality and community presence
- Emojis: Acceptable on Instagram/Facebook; use sparingly on LinkedIn
- Hashtag style: #CamelCase (not #allowercase)

- Required footer: "An Independent Licensee of the Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association"

## Press / Earned Media

### Press Release Structure
1. **FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE** (or EMBARGO date)
2. **Contact:** [Name], [title], [phone], [email]
3. **Headline:** Newsy, AP-style, present tense verb
4. **Dateline:** Berlin, Vt., [Month Day, Year] —
5. **Lede:** Who, what, when, where, why in first sentence
6. **Body:** Inverted pyramid; quotes from named executives
7. **Company boilerplate** (always the same — see brand-voice.md)

### AP Style Rules BCBS VT Follows
- "health care" = two words
- State abbreviation: Vt. (not VT in running text)
- Percentages: spell out "percent" in text; use % in headlines/data
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; numerals for 10+
- Quotes: Double quotation marks; comma/period inside
- Titles: Capitalize when before name (President and CEO Beth-Ann Roberts); lowercase after (Beth-Ann Roberts, president and CEO)

### Pitching/Media Relations Tone
- Lead with Vermont community impact, not corporate news
- Frame every story around Vermonters, not the organization
- Provide data and expert sources
- Offer local spokespeople with Vermont ties
