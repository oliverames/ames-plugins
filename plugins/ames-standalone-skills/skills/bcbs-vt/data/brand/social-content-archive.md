## Cross-Channel Engagement Patterns

### What Consistently Performs Best

1. **Leadership transitions** — New hire welcomes and retirement farewells activate personal professional networks. LinkedIn top-two performers are both this type.
2. **Financial/practical tools** — HSA/FSA Reel (Instagram's top performer) and prescription drug cost posts. People engage when you save them money.
3. **Member/employee health stories with names** — Andrew's heart attack story performed above average on both Instagram and LinkedIn. Named, real employees with real health experiences.
4. **CEO media appearances** — Beth Roberts Vermont Public / Becker's posts outperformed standard institutional content.
5. **Community charity campaigns** — Apple Days challenge (13 likes) consistently drives more engagement than operational posts because the donation mechanic activates community identity.

### What Consistently Underperforms

- Pure operational announcements (paperless billing, form updates)
- Generic health tips without Vermont or community hook
- External article links without strong editorial framing
- Posts where BCBS VT is the subject but not the storyteller

### Voice Patterns Across All Platforms

**Caption openings that work:**
- Action imperative: "Join our Fall Virtual Challenge this weekend!"
- Direct question: "Want to find out if you qualify for financial help?"
- Personal narrative: "Andrew never imagined a quiet Saturday afternoon would turn out to be a silent heart attack..."
- Community assertion: "Healthy communities don't happen by accident -- they happen when we show up for one another."
- Recognition moment: "We're thrilled to welcome..."

**Signature structural move:** State the action → state the Vermont impact
> "We'll donate $5 to @vermontadaptive for every photo shared."
> "Giving our members immediate access to cost estimates is part of our mission to make health care work better for Vermonters."

**Emoji discipline:** One or two per post, always contextual (season, subject matter). 💙 is the brand blue — use for warm brand moments, not every post.
