### The Vermont Analogy (opening hooks — confirmed pattern)

- "You can get cost estimates for repairs to your vehicle or house. Why should health care be any different?" (Cost Estimate Tool)
- "Vermonters know that some things are just better when they are stacked. Firewood dries faster in stacks, keeping us warmer in the winter. A stack of pancakes is more enticing, as maple syrup poured on top slowly trickles down the pile. But when it comes to selecting a health plan, the question of whether the deductible should be stacked is not as easy to decide." (Stacked Deductible article — exemplary Vermont hook)

### Cost Estimate Tool article (verbatim)

- "With our cost estimate tool, you can see how much your out-of-pocket cost will be for many types of health care, such as office visits, diagnostic imaging, and procedures like knee replacement."
- "The cost of a procedure like magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) can vary greatly, depending on where it is done. Larger hospitals can charge $6,000 or more for an MRI, while the cost for the same procedure at independent facility may be less than $2,000."
- "Please keep in mind that the figures you'll see are only estimates. The actual costs may vary depending on several factors, including how the provider bills us for the procedure."
- "Our new cost estimate tool greatly enhances your power as a health care consumer to shop around and make informed choices."
- "Giving our members immediate access to cost estimates is part of our mission to make health care work better for Vermonters."

**Marcey Hannon, Project Manager — blood cancer, case manager support (May 2025):**

- "One of the things that I would encourage members is to do is engage with a Blue Cross Vermont case manager as soon as possible. They're a lifeline to navigating the healthcare journey, all the way through to recovery."

**Tess Thomas, Case Manager — supporting Jim and Lyn Desmarais:**

## Advocacy Voice: VT Affordable Care Campaign

Launched Dec 2025. Coalition: BCBSVT + Northwestern Medical Center + Green Mountain Surgery Center + Vermont OPEN Imaging + Vermont Diagnostic Imaging. Site: vtaffordablecare.com

**Campaign tagline:** "Each of us can help lower the cost of our health care without sacrificing quality."

**Main headline:** "Vermonters Pay the Most in America for Healthcare"

**Key statistic:**
> "Vermonters now spend 19.6% of their income on healthcare, compared to the national average of just 7.9%. That's the highest in the country."

**Consumer empowerment frame:**
> "There are practical steps each of us can take to reduce expenses without sacrificing quality of care."

**Price comparison example:**
> MRI at UVM Medical Center: $6,520 | Northwestern Medical Center: $2,785 | Independent facility average: $1,799

**Teresa Anderson, Director of Brand and Engagement Strategies, Blue Cross VT:**
> "We really wanted to educate Vermonters on how the cost of delivery of care impacts affordability, so that people could feel empowered to make informed decisions."

**Voice note:** This campaign represents the "Evidence-Led Advocate" tonal mode. The tone is educational and coalition-minded — Blue Cross VT never attacks UVM Medical Center by name in its own voice; the price data does that work. BCBS VT frames as empowering consumers, not attacking competitors.

## What Makes BCBS VT Writing Distinctive

1. **The Vermont specificity** — Not generic Americana. Vermont's seasons, specific trails and orchards, small-town community feel. Named Vermont organizations. Actual Vermont phone numbers and town names.
2. **The analogy habit** — Complex insurance concepts grounded in Vermont everyday life: car repairs, firewood stacks, maple syrup, apple orchards.
3. **The "we're neighbors" positioning** — "Nearly 400 of us live and work here." Not a distant corporation. A local organization that employs your neighbors and volunteers after floods.
4. **Mission threading** — Every piece of content, even cost-tool explainers, comes back to "making health care work better for Vermonters."
5. **The nonprofit story** — Consistently woven in, never taken for granted. 89.7% of premiums go to claims. $250K to Vermont nonprofits. 6.2% admin ratio. This is the primary differentiator.
6. **Data with heart** — Numbers serve human benefit: "$4.9 million per day" isn't just a stat — it's proof of mission. "140 submissions to our Winter Virtual Challenge" is community proof, not just KPIs.
7. **Collaborative in language, confrontational in data** — BCBS VT's advocacy copy sounds coalition-minded; the price comparison data does the aggressive work. The organization never attacks by name — it names the numbers.
8. **The named employee/member** — Real people with titles and real health moments. Not stock photography captions. This is the strongest engagement driver across every channel.

## Blog / Editorial Structure

**Standard post architecture:**
1. Hook sentence or thesis (1–2 sentences — often a question, Vermont analogy, or bold claim)
2. Background/context paragraph
3. Named expert or employee quote (with job title)
4. Subheadings for scannable body
5. Specific tools, phone numbers, or links
6. Warm closing CTA (often: "We hope you'll...")

**Headline templates (confirmed patterns):**
- Action verb + direct object: "Finding a Mental Health Provider"
- "How to" instructional: "How to Lower Your Prescription Drug Costs"
- Benefit discovery: "Additional Benefits You Might Not Know You Have"
- Stakes/advocacy: "Affordability Matters: A Call for Change in Vermont's Health Care System"
- Superlative/milestone: "Celebrating 25 Years of Empowerment with Girls on the Run Vermont"
- Could/might softening: "Switching to a Gold Plan Could be a Smart Move"
- Named person + condition: "The Impact of Stress on Heart Health: Andrew's Story"
- Action + place: "Where to Go Hiking in Spring"

**Sentence case, not Title Case.** Action-oriented. Specific over vague.

**Five tonal modes:**
1. **Empowering Educator** — "Here's what you have. Here's how to use it." (cost tool, formulary, Be Well VT)
2. **Trusted Neighbor** — "We're from Vermont. We're not going anywhere. We're rolling up our sleeves alongside you." (community events, member letters)
3. **Evidence-Led Advocate** — "19.6% vs. 7.9%. This is not sustainable. Not for us. Not for Vermonters." (Affordability Matters, VT Affordable Care)
4. **Human Storyteller** — Name + title + their health moment. No jargon. Real employees, real members. (Andrew, Jennie, Marcey, Lyn)
5. **Celebratory Community Partner** — "25 years. Here's what that means to the people who lived it." (GOTR, Blue Sky VT Days recaps, flood recovery)
