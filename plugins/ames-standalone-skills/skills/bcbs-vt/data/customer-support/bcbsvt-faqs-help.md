# BCBS VT FAQs & Member Help Reference

Comprehensive FAQ and help content scraped from bluecrossvt.org for customer support response drafting.

## Member ID Card FAQs

**When will I receive my member ID card?**
Expect it within 7-10 business days from enrollment date.

**How often do I get a new card?**
Only when a change impacts the info on the card, or if one is requested. BCBS VT does NOT issue new cards annually.

**Lost my card — what do I do?**
Request a new one via: Member Resource Center, email customerservice@bcbsvt.com, or call (800) 247-2583 Mon–Fri 7am–6pm. New cards are always FREE.

**Appointment but no card yet?**
Access a proof of coverage letter in the Member Resource Center: Benefits/Coverage Details → "Hover here for a link to view your Outline of Coverage" → download PDF.

**Two ID cards — which is correct?**
Check the most recent effective date on the lower left side of the card.

**Can I fill a prescription without my card?**
Yes. Tell the pharmacy you're a BCBS VT member. If you don't have your member ID, they can look you up by date of birth and address.

**How to read the card:**
1. Member ID Number — last 13 digits (exclude first 3 letters) + 2-digit member line number
2. Plan Type — upper right corner; PPO suitcase icon for Open Access, EPO, Freedom Plan
3. Costs — copays, deductible, out-of-pocket max printed on front right
4. Pharmacy — "VERMONT BLUE Rx" on back with RX Group, BIN, PCN, Formulary code
5. Contact Info — on back of card

## GLP-1 Coverage Change FAQs (2026)

**What changed?**
Beginning Jan 1, 2026, BCBS VT no longer covers GLP-1 drugs FDA-approved for weight loss (Wegovy, Zepbound, Saxenda). This is a benefit exclusion — members who continue will pay full cost.

**Why?**
- Costly medications affecting premium affordability for all members
- Lasting weight loss requires ongoing long-term use, but many stop after a few months

**Who is impacted?**
Members taking GLP-1s for anything OTHER than managing Type 2 diabetes or preventing certain cardiovascular events.

**Still covered (with prior approval):**
- Type 2 diabetes: Ozempic, Mounjaro, Trulicity (and others)
- Cardiovascular risk reduction: Wegovy for adults with diagnosed CVD and obesity

**Alternative covered options:**
- Nutritional/behavioral counseling (including virtual via Amwell)
- Other weight loss medications (non-GLP-1)
- Bariatric surgery if clinically appropriate
- Employer-sponsored health and wellness benefits

**Response tone:** "We understand that unexpected changes in your drug coverage can be frustrating."

## BCBSM Affiliation FAQs

**What is the affiliation?**
Blue Cross VT became a subsidiary of Blue Cross Blue Shield of Michigan. Unlike a merger — preserves local governance, Vermont-based workforce, nonprofit status, and the BCBS VT brand name. HQ remains in Berlin, VT.

**Key reassurances for members:**
- NO changes to benefits, plan structures, or coverage
- NO changes to provider network — same doctors, same BlueCard national network
- NO new insurance cards needed
- NO impact on premiums from the affiliation itself
- Customer service and claims handled locally in Berlin, VT
- Award-winning customer service team continues unchanged

**Why affiliate?**
To leverage combined scale against well-funded for-profit competitors, improve technology, and bring innovative products to Vermont — while staying local and nonprofit.

**For providers:** Same systems, same Blue Cross VT team. Future improvements via BCBSM's technology and tools.

## Medicare Advantage Discontinuation (2026)

**What happened?**
Vermont Blue Advantage will not offer Medicare Advantage plans in 2026 (announced Oct 1, 2025).

**Why?**
"The Medicare Advantage market in Vermont has been severely affected by payers choosing to leave the Medicare Advantage market and high utilization of medical care by Vermont Blue Advantage members."

**Current options for former MA members:**
- Vermont Medigap Blue
- Vermont Blue 65 Supplement
- Blue MedicareRx (Part D)
- Explore options at Medicare.gov or work with a local agent

**Quote (Pam Getsie, VBA Plan President):** "Vermont Blue Advantage has been incredibly proud to serve Vermonters for the last 5 years."

**IMPORTANT:** Never promise MA will return. Frame as transition to supplement options.

## Enrollment Guide

**Open enrollment:** Begins November 1 each year via Vermont Health Connect.

**Qualifying life events (no need to wait):** Loss of coverage, changes in family status, changes in employment.

**Steps:**
1. Evaluate health care needs — consider last year's spending (premiums, copays, Rx, budget, anticipated needs)
2. Review plan options — use find-a-plan tool at bluecrossvt.org/our-plans/plan-comparison-tool; check subsidy eligibility at Vermont Health Connect
3. Enroll via Vermont Health Connect: info.healthconnect.vermont.gov or call (855) 899-9600

## HSA (Health Savings Accounts)

**What:** Tax-advantaged savings for qualified medical, pharmacy, dental, vision expenses. Paired with HSA-eligible health plans. Through HealthEquity.

**Key advantages:**
- Tax-deductible contributions, tax-free earnings, tax-free distributions for qualified expenses
- Money rolls over each year — yours to keep even if you change plans or retire
- Covers eligible expenses for you AND eligible family members
- Reduces taxable income

**New for 2026:** Additional Individual and Family QHPs are now HSA-eligible per federal legislation.

**Features:** Online portal (24/7), HealthEquity Visa debit card, dedicated customer service at (866) 346-5800.

**To sign up:** Complete the HSA opt-in form at bluecrossvt.org/hsa-optin. Calculator at secure.bcbsvt.com/hsaCalculator.php.

## Personal Health Support Team (Case Management)

**What:** Free, voluntary, confidential one-to-one support from registered nurses and licensed clinicians. Included in every Blue Cross VT member's benefits.

**Areas of support:**
- Pregnancy/maternal health (Better Beginnings program)
- Cancer care
- Chronic conditions (asthma, COPD, diabetes, heart disease, seizures, arthritis, Crohn's, colitis)
- Gender affirming care
- Mental health conditions
- Substance use disorder treatment and recovery
- End-of-life care

**How it works:**
- Matched with one dedicated clinician who gets to know the "whole" you
- Communication by phone, email, text via mobile app — your choice
- Calls range from 5–15 min to 45 min depending on need; business hours 8am–4:30pm (after-hours available)
- Team may proactively reach out (e.g., after hospitalization)

**How to enroll:**
- Call customer service using number on member ID card
- Call health support team: (800) 922-8778, option 3
- Email: IHMTriage@bcbsvt.com
- Secure message via Member Resource Center → Case Management

**Real examples from members:**
- COPD patient: clinician helped with manufacturer coupons for inhaler + PCP follow-up coordination
- Burn victim: clinician coordinated home health, burn clinic follow-up, replacement medical equipment, disability questions → returned to work on schedule
- Complex out-of-state surgery: clinician coordinated prior auth, provider coaching, PT coordination → successful outcome

**Closing language:** "Whether you're dealing with an existing condition or facing a new health challenge, as a Blue Cross Vermont member, you're not alone."

## Preventive Care & Affordability

**Key talking points (from CMO Tom Weigel):**
- Chronic conditions (diabetes, heart disease) account for large share of spending but many are preventable
- Skipping routine screenings → late diagnosis → higher costs → worse outcomes
- BCBS VT investments: free preventive screenings, Be Well at Work, 625,000+ telehealth visits (2024), home drug infusion therapy, Personal Health Support Team
- "We must flip the script from reactive care to proactive care. From treating illness to supporting wellness."
- Prevention benefits employers (fewer claims, greater productivity, stronger teams) and families (peace of mind, financial protection)
