## Talking Points (in recommended order)

The campaign organizes affordability advice into four buckets. When drafting content, lead with one bucket rather than mixing them all into a single piece.

**1. Compare your options and save**
- Procedures: hospital vs. accredited outpatient or ambulatory surgery centers (often significantly less expensive at ASCs)
- Medical imaging: network and community hospitals vs. independent facilities
- Generic medications vs. brand-name drugs (ask the provider)

**2. Use cost-comparison resources**
- Reduce out-of-pocket costs by avoiding surprise bills and planning effectively
- Featured tools (partner co-publishers):
  - Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont
  - Green Mountain Surgery Center
  - Northwestern Medical Center
  - Vermont OPEN Imaging

**3. Choose the right care option**
- Urgent care for moderate conditions (lower cost and shorter waits than ER; often offers imaging and lab testing)
- Primary care for non-emergent issues (cheaper than urgent care, knows your history, often same- or next-day appointments)
- Emergency room for serious, potentially life-threatening conditions only

**4. Get things in writing**
- Ask facilities for written estimates beforehand
- Itemized bills after the service to confirm charges
- Ask questions about care plans, medications, follow-ups, home equipment, support resources

**5. Maximize your health-plan benefits**
- Understand covered benefits in advance (Outline of Coverage, customer service)
- Use in-network providers (more affordable, avoids surprise expenses)
- Take advantage of $0 or low-cost screenings and preventive care

## Co-Branding Compliance (CRITICAL — do not skip)

Per `data/brand/authoritative-brand-style-guide-2025-10.md` § Co-Branding (Brand Style Guide Oct 2025, p. 11), any co-branded asset must:

- **Lead time:** Build **6–8 weeks** into the project timeline for BCBSA approval
- **Approval validity:** 3 years; re-apply within that window
- **Prominence:** Blue Cross VT must be **equal to or more prominent** than partner names/logos in size, color, placement, and visual weight
- **Independent-status disclosure:** "[Partner Name] is an independent company providing [service] on behalf of Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont"
- **What must NOT be implied:** that the partner is licensed by BCBSA, affiliated with a Licensee, or part of the Blue Cross Blue Shield System
- **Prohibited language:** "endorsed by," "in partnership with," "endorsement," "sponsorship"
- **Within 6 months of use:** submit samples of co-branded materials to BCBSA via Brand and Engagement Strategies for compliance review

## Voice Notes Specific to This Campaign

- This is **not a political campaign** — it's framed as practical, neighborly guidance. Keep tone consistent with the Brand Voice Summary.
- The 19.6%/7.9% stat is a reframe, not an attack on providers. Even when discussing cost variation between hospitals and ASCs, the tone is informational ("often significantly less"), not accusatory.
- Use "Vermonters" not "consumers" or "customers" wherever possible.
- "Health care" (two words) when describing the actions of providers and patients; "healthcare" (one word) only when describing the system or industry. See authoritative writing guide.
- Always source-attribute the headline statistic. Never quote 19.6%/7.9% without the WalletHub-July-2025 attribution.
