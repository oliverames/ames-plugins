# BCBS VT Customer Research Context

## Organization
Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont — Vermont's only local nonprofit health plan, 230,000+ members.
Full brand guidelines: `~/.claude/brand-voice-guidelines.md`

## Member Demographics & Context
- Covers ~1/3 of all Vermonters
- Vermont population: ~650,000 (small, rural state)
- Members span: individuals, families, employers (1–100 employees), Medicare supplement
- Vermont is aging population with rural access challenges
- Significant mental health demand — 625,000+ telehealth visits in 2024

## Key Member Pain Points
1. **Affordability** — VT households spend 19.6% of income on healthcare (#1 nationally). Premium costs are the primary member concern.
2. **Provider access in rural areas** — finding specialists, especially mental health providers, in rural Vermont
3. **Understanding benefits** — members often don't know about available tools (cost estimate tool, Be Well Vermont, pharmacy consultations)
4. **Medicare transition confusion** — Vermont Blue Advantage MA plans discontinued 2026; members need guidance on supplement options
5. **GLP-1 medication coverage changes** — emotionally charged topic (Jan 2026 change)
6. **Prescription drug costs** — members need help navigating formulary tiers and finding lower-cost alternatives

## Competitive Landscape
- BCBS VT is the dominant insurer — only local nonprofit health plan
- MVP Health Care (now part of Point32Health) is the primary competitor
- UVM Health Network is the dominant provider system (and a key cost driver — BCBS VT advocates for price transparency but never attacks UVM by name)
- Vermont has a regulated insurance market through Green Mountain Care Board (GMCB)
- GMCB approves/modifies rate increase requests (e.g., cut BCBS VT's 23.5% request to 9.6% for 2026)

## Service Awards & Trust Signals
- 2024 Call Center of the Year (SQM, from 500+ North American call centers)

- Gold Bell Seal for Workplace Mental Health (2025)
- Blue Crew employee volunteer program (8 hours paid volunteer time annually)

## Financial Context (for escalation/complaint handling)
- $62.1 million net loss in 2024; cumulative ~$152M loss over 4 years
- $30M loan from BCBS Michigan at 8% interest
- AM Best downgraded to C++ (Marginal)
- Financial recovery underway — profitable through Q3 2025
- Act 55 of 2025 helped: pharmaceutical/hospital infusion drug reimbursement cap
- UVM Health Network contract renegotiated with reduced reimbursement + value-based mechanisms
- Tone for financial questions: candid, not defensive — "We know how complicated and frustrating health care can be."
