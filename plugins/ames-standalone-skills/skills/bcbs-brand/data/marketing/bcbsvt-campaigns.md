# BCBS VT Campaign Planning Reference

## Organization Context
Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Vermont — Vermont's only local, not-for-profit health plan.
Social Media Strategist: Oliver Ames. Full brand guidelines: `~/.claude/brand-voice-guidelines.md`

## Active Campaign: VT Affordable Care (Dec 2025–present)
- **Coalition:** BCBS VT + Northwestern Medical Center + Green Mountain Surgery Center + Vermont OPEN Imaging + Vermont Diagnostic Imaging
- **Site:** vtaffordablecare.com
- **Tagline:** "Each of us can help lower the cost of our health care without sacrificing quality."
- **Headline:** "Vermonters Pay the Most in America for Healthcare"
- **Key stat:** 19.6% of income on healthcare vs. 7.9% national average
- **Frame:** Consumer empowerment — "you have more affordable, quality options"
- **Voice rule:** NEVER attack competitors/hospitals by name. The price data does the work.
- **Price comparison example:** MRI at UVM Medical Center: $6,520 | Northwestern: $2,785 | Independent avg: $1,799

## Seasonal Campaign Framework: Blue Sky VT Days

**Umbrella brand:** Blue Sky VT Days℠ (four seasonal challenges)
**Universal social mechanic:** "Tag Blue Cross Vermont in photos on Facebook or Instagram (or email events@bcbsvt.com) and we'll donate $5 to [charity] for every photo."

| Season | Event | Charity Partner | Hashtag |

## Engagement Patterns (LinkedIn)
1. Leadership transitions (welcomes/farewells) = 5-10x baseline engagement
2. CEO media appearances = consistent second tier
3. Member/employee health stories with names = third tier
4. Seasonal events = lowest engagement on LinkedIn (better on Instagram/Facebook)

## Engagement Patterns (Instagram)
1. Financial/practical tools with clear action step (HSA/FSA Reel = 39 likes, all-time high)
2. Seasonal challenges with charity donation mechanic
3. Named employee/member health stories
4. Pure operational announcements = lowest engagement
